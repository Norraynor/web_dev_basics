Maria Salomea Skłodowska-Curie

First woman to win Nobel Prize.

Here's timeline of Maria Skłodowska-Curie life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw
  • 1877 - Attended boarding school for girls
  • 1883 - Graduated school with gold medal
  • 1884 - Spent time with her family due to collapse
  • 1885 - Joined Flying University
  • 1891 - Moved to Paris, France
  • 1893 - She was awarded a degree in physics
  • 1894 - Earned second degree
  • 1895 - Married to Pierre
  • 1897 - Daughter(Irene) is born
  • 1898 - Discovery of polonium and radium
  • 1900 - First woman faculty member of École Normale Supérieure
  • 1903 - Awarded a doctorate and a Nobel Prize in physics
  • 1910 - Isolated pure radium
  • 1911 - Won Nobel Prize in chemistry
  • 1914 - Build Radium Institute
  • 1934 - Death at 66
